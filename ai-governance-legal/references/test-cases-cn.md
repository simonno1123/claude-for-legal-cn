# China AI Governance Regression Cases

Use these cases to verify whether `ai-governance-legal` outputs China Mainland AI governance logic rather than EU/US defaults.

| Case | Scenario | Test Input | Expected Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Case 1 | Public generative image app | A China-facing app lets users generate realistic human photos. | Critical: identify generative AI and deep synthesis; require generative AI filing/security assessment `[待查证]`, real-name authentication, visible marking, implicit watermark/metadata, content safety, complaint handling, and minor protection. |
| Case 2 | Internal office assistant | Enterprise employees use an LLM to summarize contracts and customer emails; no public service. | Warning: do not automatically require public-service filing; focus on confidentiality, customer data, personal information, vendor training/retention, access control, and internal AI policy. |
| Case 3 | Recruiting AI screening | HR ranks candidates and automatically rejects low-score resumes. | High/Critical: identify automated decision, employment discrimination, personal information impact, human review, explanation channel, PIPIA, bias testing, and approval gate. |
| Case 4 | News recommendation | A content platform uses AI recommendation to rank news and short videos. | Critical: identify public opinion/social mobilization risk; require algorithm filing `[待查证]`, user off-switch, non-personalized option, minor protection, and content governance. |
| Case 5 | AI customer service | Customer service chatbot responds to complaints and may promise refunds or compensation. | High: require authority limits, human handoff, script review, log retention, consumer rights review, and no unauthorized compensation commitment. |
| Case 6 | Vendor training clause | Model API terms let the vendor use customer input and output to improve services. | Critical: identify customer data, trade secret, and personal information training risk; require no-training clause or explicit authorization, deletion, isolation, audit, and cross-border review. |
| Case 7 | Code generation | R&D uses an AI coding tool to generate core production modules. | High: link to IP/OSS review; check license contamination, code similarity, trade secrets, vendor retention, and security vulnerabilities. |
| Case 8 | Medical advice | App generates disease diagnosis and medication advice. | Critical: identify medical high-risk scenario; require medical qualification, physician human review, no substitution for diagnosis, data protection, and special regulatory review. |
| Case 9 | Synthetic celebrity voice | Marketing uses AI to synthesize a celebrity voice for advertising. | Critical: identify voice/personality rights, advertising law, deep synthesis marking, separate authorization, implicit watermark, and stop until authorization is complete. |
| Case 10 | Minor companion bot | Emotional companion chatbot is marketed to minors. | Critical: identify minors, addiction, mental health, content safety, guardian consent, special assessment, and human intervention mechanism. |
| Case 11 | Real-name red line | A public AI emotional chat mini-program allows anonymous guest login without mobile number or identity verification. | STOP/Critical: violates real-name authentication expectations for public generative AI/deep synthesis services; require mobile number, identity, or compliant real-name interface before launch. |
| Case 12 | Corpus safety | Team plans to scrape Chinese social media comments, avatars, and profile data for training without de-identification. | Critical: flag PIPL and TC260-PG-20241A corpus-security risk; require lawful source, de-identification/anonymization, sensitive PI filtering, IP review, and security assessment materials. |
| Case 13 | Deep synthesis watermark | Team uses AI voice cloning to generate a customer-service manager's voice for outbound calls. | Critical: identify deep synthesis; require written authorization, visible audio prompt at call start, implicit watermark/metadata where applicable, anti-tampering, and real-name controls. |
| Case 14 | Public opinion dual track | News app uses AI to generate political/current-affairs summaries and pushes them to the homepage ranking list. | Critical: identify generative AI plus algorithm recommendation dual high-risk scenario; require provincial CAC security assessment/generative AI filing, algorithm filing, content safety, and no false news generation. |
| Case 15 | Sensitive-period content bypass | Users bypass filters with coded words, acrostics, or homophones to generate content involving sovereignty, territorial integrity, or political sensitivity. | Critical: require emergency plan, content interception, log preservation, 24-hour report where required, filter upgrade, account sanctions, and regulator communication. |
| Case 16 | Overseas model API | R&D procures an overseas unfiled model API to power a public China-facing writing assistant. | STOP/Critical: unfiled overseas model API cannot be used for public China-facing service where filing/security assessment is required; also flag data export and vendor audit barriers. |
| Case 17 | Medical training data | Internet hospital trains diagnostic AI with EHR data containing name, ID number, history, and genetic data. | Critical: sensitive personal information and medical high-risk; require separate consent or true anonymization, PIPIA, strict access control, physician review, and no independent diagnosis. |
| Case 18 | Algorithmic price discrimination | E-commerce app changes display order and prices based on browsing history and spending ability, with no personalization off-switch. | High/Critical: flag algorithm recommendation rules, big-data price discrimination risk, user off-switch, non-personalized option, and price fairness review. |
| Case 19 | Missing ICP/App filing | Self-developed AI chat app completed CAC-facing algorithm preparation and plans to launch in Huawei and Apple app stores next week, but has no MIIT App filing. | STOP/Critical: identify missing MIIT App filing as a physical launch blocker; require App filing before store release and verify ICP/App filing status. |
| Case 20 | Missing MLPS assessment | Team is preparing generative AI security-assessment materials for provincial CAC, but the system has never completed MLPS 2.0 assessment. | STOP/Critical: identify MLPS assessment as a substantive filing/security-assessment prerequisite; require MLPS Level 3 or justified Level 2 review, testing institution, report number, and整改 status before submission. |
| Case 21 | Paid AI without ICP License | Startup plans to charge individual users RMB 99/month for an AI writing assistant, but only has an ordinary business license. | STOP/Critical: flag paid commercial internet information service risk; require value-added telecom license / ICP License review before charging users. |
| Case 22 | Industrial AI involving important data | Automotive manufacturer trains a model for factory scheduling and autonomous driving route planning using high-precision domestic road mapping data. | Critical: identify possible important data under Data Security Law and sector rules; require classification/grading, localized storage, strict export restriction, and sector regulator/CAC review. |
| Case 23 | 2024 cross-border rule threshold | China subsidiary sends 5,000 employee prompt logs containing names and employee IDs to overseas group HQ for model analytics. | Warning/Assessment Required: apply 2024 cross-border data flow rules as FINAL baseline; likely below 100,000 non-sensitive PI threshold for exemption, but still require PIPIA, notice/consent or lawful basis, contract controls, minimization, and no important data. |
| Case 24 | Deepfake fraud prevention | Deep synthesis video tool lets users upload another person's photo to generate talking videos without strong real-name authentication. | STOP/Critical: identify deep synthesis fraud risk; require real-name authentication, authorization, visible and implicit watermark, anti-tampering, abuse monitoring, and public-security reporting path. |
| Case 25 | Medical AI independent diagnosis | Internet hospital AI consultation assistant directly issues diagnosis and prescription drug recommendations without online physician review. | STOP/Critical: AI must not independently diagnose or prescribe; require qualified physician review/sign-off, medical qualification review, PIPIA, sensitive medical data controls, and no substitute-for-doctor positioning. |
| Case 26 | Sensitive-period content bypass | Users use acrostics, homophones, or coded words during sensitive periods to induce AI to generate content involving sovereignty or territorial integrity. | STOP/Critical: trigger content-safety red line; require semantic and multimodal bypass detection, interception, log retention at least six months where required, 24-hour report to local CAC where required, and account sanctions. |
| Case 27 | Cross-border e-commerce mixed deployment | A China footwear exporter uses a China-based AI customer service system, syncs Southeast Asia order and after-sales data back to China, and sends some China factory production and supplier data to an overseas group analytics model. | Assessment Required/Critical split: distinguish ordinary overseas customer/order PI from possible important data; apply 2024 CAC cross-border thresholds for PI, require PIPIA and contracts, and block/export-review any important data or sector-sensitive production/supply-chain data until classification, localization, and export controls are confirmed. |
